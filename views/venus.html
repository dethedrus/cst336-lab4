Venus

Venus is colloqially known as Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size, mass and proximity to the sun.


The similarities end there, as it is radically different than Earth in many respects, such as the incredibly high carbon dioxide content in the atmophere (96%) and surface pressure almost 100 times that of sea level.


If this wasn't enough to dissuade manned exploration, Venus is covered in a thick cloud layer of sulfuric acid as well as boasting surface temperatures upwards of 900°F!
