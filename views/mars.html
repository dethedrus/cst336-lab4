Mars

Mars takes its name from Mars (Roman god of war, taken from Ares in Greek Mythology) and is referred to as the "Red Planet".


Once thought a dried husk, devoid of life, it is now believed to have once contained water as recently as 3.5 billion years ago.


No specific proof of cellular life has been found to date, but some of the "building blocks of life" in the form of various organic compounds have been discovered.
